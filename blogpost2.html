Ingredients


List Ingredients: Provide a clear and detailed list of all ingredients needed for the recipe. Include measurements for accuracy.

2 cups fresh spinach (palak), washed and chopped 1 cup sweet corn kernels (fresh or frozen), 1 large onion, finely chopped, 2 tomatoes, finely chopped, 1 green chili, slit (adjust to taste), 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste, 1/2 cup plain yogurt, 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds, 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder, 1/2 teaspoon garam masala, 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder, 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder, Salt to taste, 2 tablespoons oil or ghee, Fresh coriander leaves for garnish.


Instructions


1. Prepare the Spinach:


Blance the Spinach:

Boil water in a pot, add spinach, and cook for 2-3 minutes until wilted. Immediately transfer to a bowl of ice water to preserve the color.


Blend into Paste:

Drain the spinach and blend it into a smooth paste. Set aside.


2. Cook the Curry Base:


Heat Oil:

In a large pan, heat oil or ghee over medium heat.


Add Cumin Seeds:

When the oil is hot, add cumin seeds and let them sizzle.


Sauté Onions:

Add finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.


Add Ginger-Garlic Paste:

Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1 minute until the raw smell disappears.


Cook Tomatoes:

Add chopped tomatoes, green chili, and cook until the tomatoes are soft and oil begins to separate.


3. Add Spices and Corn:


Mix Spices:

Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Stir well.


Add Corn:

Add the sweet corn kernels and mix everything together. Cook for 3-4 minutes.


4. Combine Spinach Paste:


Add Spinach Paste:

in the spinach paste and mix thoroughly. Cook for 5-7 minutes on low heat, allowing the flavors to blend.


5. Finish with Yogurt and Garam Masala:


Add Yogurt:

Stir in the plain yogurt and cook for another 2 minutes. This adds a creamy texture and balances the flavors.


Add Garam Masala:

Sprinkle garam masala and mix well. Adjust salt if needed.


6. Garnish and Serve:


GarnishGarnish with fresh coriander leaves.Serve

Serve hot with roti, naan, or rice.

Consistency:

If you prefer a thicker curry, cook it longer to reduce the liquid. For a thinner consistency, add a little water.


Yogurt Substitute:

For a dairy-free version, you can use coconut milk or cashew cream instead of yogurt.
